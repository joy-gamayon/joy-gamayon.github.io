To describe HTML, CSS, and Javascript's connection, I will create an analogy in order for you to have a better understanding about their connections to each other.
In this analogy, it would be about our dining experience in a restaurant relating to HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Let's say I invited you to a fancy fusion restaurant. The menu (which we consider as a HTML) lays out all the dishes and drinks available for us, from appetizers to desserts, in a structured and organize manner. On the menu, each dish is listed with its name, description and price, just like HTML defines the structure and content of a webpage.


Then CSS, would be considered as the restaurant's ambiance, which adds to our dining experience with its decorations, dim lighting, elegant table settings, and soothing background music. This ambiance is like CSS, as it adds colour, style and layout to the restaurant to create a memorable dining experience. Just like CSS enhances the HTML content, making it visually appealing, and attractive to diners.


In order to create a memorable dining experience, the restaurant must interact to their customers (you and I) through actions or service. And that's where Javascript comes in, as JS would make the interaction take place which brings the restaurant to life. It's like the skilled waitstaff who take our orders, deliver our food and drinks, and ensure that we have a fantastic dining experience by giving a high quality service. Like the Javascript itself that adds the interactive and dynamic elements to the webpage.
